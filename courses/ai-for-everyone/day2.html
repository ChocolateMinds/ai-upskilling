Learn: The CLEAR Prompt Methodology

A proven framework for creating effective prompts that get consistent, high-quality results.

CLEAR Method Breakdown:

  • C - Context: Provide background information
  • L - Length: Specify desired response length
  • E - Examples: Give examples when helpful
  • A - Audience: Define who the response is for
  • R - Role: Tell AI what role to take
Example CLEAR Prompt:
"You are a professional email writer (Role). Help me write a follow-up email to a client who hasn't responded to our proposal (Context). Keep it to 2-3 paragraphs (Length). The tone should be friendly but professional, suitable for a busy executive (Audience). Here's an example of our usual tone: [previous email example] (Examples)."

Session 2: The 4 Building Blocks of Effective Prompts

45 minutes

Master the framework that separates amateur prompts from professional-grade AI interactions.

Core Framework: R.C.T.C Method

The 4 Building Blocks:
  1. Role – Who should the AI be?
  2. Context – What's the situation?
  3. Task – What exactly do you need?
  4. Constraints – Any rules, style, or limits?
Example: Bad vs. Good Prompt
❌ Bad: "Write a marketing email."
✅ Good: "You are an experienced copywriter. Write a 150-word email promoting a free AI workshop for small business owners. Use a friendly tone, include 3 bullet points about benefits, and end with a strong call-to-action."

Advanced Techniques

  • Zero-shot prompting: Getting results without examples
  • Few-shot prompting: Providing 1-3 examples to guide output
  • Chain-of-thought: Ask AI to think step-by-step
  • System messages: Set AI personality and constraints
Pro Tip: Always ask ChatGPT to "think step by step" for complex problems - this activates its reasoning capabilities and often produces better results.
